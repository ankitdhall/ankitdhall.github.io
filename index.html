Biography

I work on Computer Vision and Machine Learning at Seervision. In 2019, I completed my graduate studies at ETH-Zurich, exploring research areas at the intersection of Computer Vision and Machine Learning.

I worked on my Master thesis at Andreas Krause's group, Learning & Adaptive Systems on learning representations for images with hierarchical labels under the supervision of Anastasia Makarova, Octavian Eugen-Ganea and Dario Pavllo.

Previously, I was a research intern at nuTonomy (an Aptiv company), Singapore working on perception and machine learning for autonomous driving.

I was the Head of Perception at Academic Motorsport Association of Zurich (AMZ) for the Formula Student Driverless, Season 2018-2019. In 2017-2018, I contributed to the Computer Vision package. In 2018 we were declared Champions at Formula Student Italy and Formula Student Germany and in 2019 at Formula Student East and Formula Student Germany. Here's a video of "gotthard driverless" cruising autonomously at the Hockenheimring from FSG'18.

In 2015, I had the opportunity to work with James Davis and Rajan Vaish during my sophomore year. I spent a wonderful summer of 2016 with Wolfram Burgard's research group at the University of Freiburg, Germany funded by DAAD. I completed my Bachelor's at VIT University studying Computer Science under the guidance of P. Nithyadarsini and M. Sivagami. I completed my thesis at K.M. Krishna's lab at Robotics Research Center, IIIT-Hyderabad.
